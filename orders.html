The Orders

There is perhaps no other property in classical architecture as distinctive and well-known as the three orders. In architecture, Order is the label we use for a description of a particular style of building; we can think of the classical orders as simply decorative variants of the ancient Post and Lintel system, but their particular attraction lies in the simple, but beautiful, innovations made in classical antiquity. This page will present the three orders of classical architecture - the Dorian, Ionian and Corinthian - and give summaries and examples of each in turn.
